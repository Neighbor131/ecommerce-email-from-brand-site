# Layout Patterns

Distilled from the shared Figma email-template library.

Use this file to choose layout patterns based on email purpose.

## Core Principle

Treat the reference system as a module and archetype library.

Use it to answer:

- what kind of structure fits this email?
- how many sections should it carry?
- how product-heavy should it be?
- where should the CTA live?
- when should imagery do the selling?

Do not import another brand's tone, colors, or campaign logic.

## Layout Archetypes

### 1. Hero-Led Conversion

Best for:

- welcome
- promo
- launch
- category push

Typical sequence:

- preheader or simple header
- strong hero
- short value statement
- one clear CTA
- optional supporting products or category cards
- compact footer

Use when:

- one idea should dominate
- the reader should understand the offer quickly
- imagery carries strong selling weight

### 2. Category Or Routine Selector

Best for:

- welcome
- category discovery
- bundle / routine builder
- merchandising emails

Typical sequence:

- brief intro
- 2-4 category or routine cards
- one follow-up CTA row
- optional best-seller support

Use when:

- the brand has multiple shopping paths
- the user needs help choosing where to start

### 3. Product Grid Driver

Best for:

- bestseller
- promo
- launch follow-up
- bundle or collection push

Typical sequence:

- short hero or heading
- product hero card
- double or triple product cards
- CTA row

Use when:

- product density is part of the selling strategy
- comparison and browsing support clicks

Avoid when:

- the brand requires strong editorial buildup before shopping

### 4. Editorial Story-Led

Best for:

- editorial
- founder or brand-story emails
- education
- content-led launches

Typical sequence:

- headline
- full image
- long-form copy blocks
- split image-text rows
- limited CTA count

Use when:

- context is needed before conversion
- the brand relies on narrative or education

### 5. Offer / Utility Focused

Best for:

- promo
- code-based offers
- urgency emails
- reactivation

Typical sequence:

- alert bar or promo box
- headline and offer
- CTA
- selected supporting products

Use when:

- the incentive is verified and central

Avoid when:

- the offer is unverified or weak

### 6. Long Campaign Stack

Best for:

- major launches
- seasonal campaigns
- mixed editorial + merchandising sends

Typical sequence:

- hero
- value statement
- multiple image/text modules
- product cards
- multiple CTA points
- full footer

Use when:

- there is enough genuine campaign material to sustain the length

Avoid when:

- the email is being padded with filler

## CTA Guidance

- Use one dominant CTA path for most conversion-first emails.
- Secondary CTAs can support navigation, but should not compete with the primary action.
- Split CTA rows work best when the user genuinely has two equivalent paths.
- Too many CTAs without hierarchy weakens clicks.

## Product Density Guidance

- Low density:
  best for welcome, editorial, single-product launch
- Medium density:
  best for category push, promo, routine builder
- High density:
  best for bestseller roundups and collection-heavy promos

## Image Usage Guidance

- Full-bleed or full-width imagery works best when the brand has strong campaign visuals.
- Product cards work best when users are likely to compare or browse.
- Split image-text blocks are useful when education and commerce need to coexist.
- Use real site assets whenever possible. Use generated imagery only when a real asset gap exists.

## Purpose To Archetype Suggestions

- welcome: Hero-Led Conversion or Category Or Routine Selector
- promo: Hero-Led Conversion or Offer / Utility Focused
- launch: Hero-Led Conversion or Long Campaign Stack
- bestseller: Product Grid Driver
- bundle: Category Or Routine Selector or Product Grid Driver
- editorial: Editorial Story-Led
- winback: Offer / Utility Focused or Hero-Led Conversion
